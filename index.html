Crime in San Francisco

This page is dedicated to investigating and exploring patterns on SF crime data during the COVID-19 pandemic. The dataset contains x crimes from the period of 2019 to 2022. The data contains temporal and spatial information which is used to uncover and highlight some of these patterns. This project has hand-picked a select few personal focus crimes to explore in more depth, these include: Arson, Burglary, Drug Violation, Embezzlement, Forgery and Counterfeiting, Larceny Theft, Missing Person, Motor Vehicle theft, Prostitution, Robbery, Stolen Property, Vandalism, Weapon Laws

Scroll down to explore different perspectives on the data.

What crimes increased the most during the pandemic

This visualization compares the number of crimes in each category before and during the pandemic. It highlights which crime types saw the largest increases. From the plot, burglary and motor vehicle theft stands out with a significant increase during the pandemic compared to other crimes.

Where did these crimes change the most?

By analyzing the ratio of the count of crime incidents in the preCOVID period to the count of crime incidents during the COVID period, we can identify the most affected districts and dive deeper into the characteristics of these areas. From the visualization, it is clear that the districts: Northern, Richmond and Park (which lie in the north-west part of the city) experienced the biggest increase in burglaries, while at the same time the districts: Bayview and Central (which lie in the south-east part of the city) experienced a growth in motor vehicle thefts.

Did the time in a week of the crime change due to COVID-19?

Looking at the hourly distribution throughout the week in the relevant districts, we can see what patterns in the occurencies of the crimes has formed along with the increased frequency due to the pandemic. A general observation that can be easily spotted is that, because of more incidents during the COVID-19, the distributions are smoother and display trends rather than sharp pikes or drops. When analzying burglary patterns, one can notice that in the Park, Northern and Richmond districts the high activity periods span from the afternoon thoroughout the night. While in general in all those three districts more incidents are registered during the weekend, in Richmond Saturday has relatively lower number of burglaries.
